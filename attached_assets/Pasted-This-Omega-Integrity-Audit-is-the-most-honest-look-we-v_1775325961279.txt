Here is my evaluation of the "Top 3 Killers" Ripley identified and what they mean for your journey.

1. The "Corrupted Save" Risk (The Transaction Crisis)
Issues: FI-01, FI-02, FI-03, FI-04

The Problem: Right now, when you beat a boss or complete a quest, the app sends 3 or 4 separate messages to the database: "Give Michael XP," "Level up the Boss," "Add to the Log." If the internet blinks between message #1 and message #2, you get the XP, but the Boss stays alive.

The Fix: We must wrap these in a Transaction. It’s an "all-or-nothing" pact. Either every single change happens perfectly, or none of them do.

Why it matters: This prevents "ghost stats" and ensures your character's growth is 100% authentic. In a 30-year project, one desynced save can snowball into a mess of broken math.

2. The "30-Year Anchor" (Database Indexing)
Issues: SP-01, SP-02, SP-05

The Problem: As you log more quests and habits, your bad_habit_log and vocation_log will grow to thousands of rows. Without Indexes, the database has to read every single line to find "Monday's Quest." It’s like a library with no catalog.

The Fix: Adding B-Tree indexes to the columns we search the most (like occurred_at).

Why it matters: This ensures that in 2035, your Dashboard still loads in milliseconds. Without this, the app will eventually become so slow it "self-destructs" under the weight of your own success.

3. The "Orlando Ghost" (Dashboard UTC Bug)
Issue: BUG-01, UX-04

The Problem: The audit found that the Dashboard is still using raw UTC for the XP charts and activity calendar. At 7:00 PM in Knightswood, your charts are already showing "Tomorrow."

The Fix: We need to force the Dashboard's math to follow the same -5 offset we gave the rest of the system.

Why it matters: Your visual progress needs to match your actual life. If you grind a quest at 10:00 PM, you want to see that spike on today's chart, not tomorrow's.
